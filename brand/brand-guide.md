## § 03 · Typography

### Three Typefaces (All Google Fonts)

#### 01 · Newsreader (Italic)
- **Role:** Voice, wordmark, headlines, overlays, story openers
- **Usage:** Italic 400 only. Never roman, never bold.
- **Weights:** 300–700 available, but only 400 italic is used by Barbon
- **Tracking:** Natural (–1.2 to –0.2px at display sizes)
- **Sizes:** 56px–72px display, 22px–30px subheads

#### 02 · Manrope (500 primary)
- **Role:** Mark, labels, navigation, captions, body copy, structure
- **Primary weight:** 500 Medium (95% of use)
- **Available weights:** 300 (delicate small text), 400, 500 ✓, 600 (emphasis only), 700
- **Tracking:** 2–4px when uppercase (labels, sub-marks)
- **Sizes:** 14–17px body, 9–11px labels

#### 03 · JetBrains Mono
- **Role:** Archive, reference, metadata, precision signals
- **Usage:** Reference numbers, shoot codes, folio stamps, dates
- **Weight:** 400 Regular (use sparingly)
- **Tracking:** 1.4–2px uppercase
- **Sizes:** 8–10px indices, 9–11px metadata

### Web Type Scale

| Size | Family | Example | Use |
|------|--------|---------|-----|
| 56–72px | Newsreader italic | Hero headline | Display, section openers |
| 36–48px | Newsreader italic | Taglines, titles | Pull quotes, straplines |
| 22–30px | Newsreader italic | Subheads | Intro text, captions |

| 9–11px | Manrope 500 uppercase | LABELS | Navigation, sub-marks |

## § 04 · Voice & Language

### The North Star
"The friend you'd ask before you list."

Barbon writes the way you'd talk to a friend who knows what they're doing — calm, useful, never selling. Trusted advisor first, impressive second.

### Two Registers (Same voice, different warmth)

#### Advisor Voice (Estate agents, default)
- Confident, professional, helpful colleague
- Full sentences, plain words, first names
- Restraint: 55 | Warmth: 60 | Wit: 25
- Sample: "Send the address and the price. We'll come back with a plan, the weather window, and a fixed fee — by tomorrow."

#### Place Voice (Hosts, sensory, slower)
- Sensory, place-led, details matter
- The things that make a space real (light at 4pm, the kettle, the lane)
- Restraint: 30 | Warmth: 75 | Wit: 40
- Sample: "Three nights in a converted dairy in Herefordshire. The bath fills slowly. The kettle is always on."

### Five Principles

01. **Useful before clever.** Every sentence should help the reader do something — pick a date, picture the result, send the brief. If it's only there to sound nice, cut it.

02. **First names, full sentences.** "Hi James" is fine. "Dear Sirs" is not. But we write in full sentences — texting tone, edited.

03. **Notice the design.** We see the building, not just the listing. Materials, proportions, era. A friend who happens to read architecture.

04. **Make the ask easy.** When we ask for the business, we make it small. Send a postcode and we'll be back in a day. No forms. No funnels.

05. **Midlands, openly.** Birmingham is not the address — it's the identity. We name Edgbaston, Solihull, Stratford, the Black Country.

### Words to Use (With Intent)

Made with care · Told well · Worth the time · A considered approach · By tomorrow · Send the address · A fixed fee · The morning light · A working fireplace · A 1930s semi · Photography, video, & a portfolio · A small studio · From Birmingham · Across the Midlands

### Never Use (Retired in v2)

**Retired (signalled wrong voice):** Quietly · Cinematic · Luxury

**Never:** Stunning · Amazing · Premium · Bespoke · High-end · Exclusive · World-class · Award-winning · Solutions · Deliverables · Reach out · Cozy retreat · Home from home · Content · Assets

## § 05 · The Four Disciplines

### D·01 Residential
- **Audience:** Estate agents, listings £300k–£500k+
- **Status:** Core · Today & ongoing
- **What:** Photography (25–40 images), video (90s main, 30s social, silent portal cuts), printed portfolio
- **Turnaround:** Fixed fee. Plan within 24 hours of postcode.
- **The ask:** "Send the address and the price. We'll be back with a plan by tomorrow."

### D·02 Hospitality
- **Audience:** Hosts, Airbnb, serviced accommodation, Plum Guide
- **Status:** Core · Today & ongoing
- **What:** Photography (30–50 images), video (60s place film, 15s social), printed one-pager
- **Approach:** Two days on-site. Find the best light hour. Make the place *feel* before they book.
- **The ask:** "Send the listing. We'll come back with a plan by tomorrow."

### D·03 Architecture
- **Audience:** Architectural practices, completed projects, Midlands-based
- **Status:** Growing into (not instead of, alongside)
- **What:** Photography for practice site, video for build record. "An architect's eye, slowed down."
- **The ask:** Two days, four elevations, the right hour for each.

### D·04 Interiors
- **Audience:** Interior designers, makers, staged interiors, fit-out
- **Status:** Growing into
- **What:** Portfolio photography. "Light, material, surface. The way a hand rests on a chair."
- **The ask:** A slow look at the rooms you've made.

## § 07 · Photo Direction

### Style Guide
- **Lighting:** Real light, not dramatic. Morning side-light preferred for residential. Golden hour for hosts.
- **Composition:** Details matter. Show the building's era, materials, proportions. Walnut floors, sash windows, working fireplaces.
- **Mood:** Calm, considered, unhurried. Not cinematic, not aspirational. Like a friend walking you through.
- **Colour:** Warm but natural. Respect the actual colours of wood, brick, stone. No heavy colour grading.

### Sub-mark Placement
One sub-mark per shoot, bottom-left on every image (10px mono text, oxblood rule divider).
- Barbon — **Residential**
- Barbon — **Hospitality**
- Barbon — **Architecture**
- Barbon — **Interiors**

## § 08 · Usage Rules

### Oxblood (Never a fill)
- 1px horizontal rules separating sections
- Index numbers (№ 014)
- One accent word per section
- Sub-mark discipline label divider
- Do NOT use as button fills, background washes, or large blocks

### Bone & Ink (Default pair)
- Bone = background / paper
- Ink = text / rules / primary visual weight
- These two form 95% of the layout

### White space
- Generous. 120–160px padding common between sections.
- The space is the brand. Let it breathe.

### Imagery
- Real photographs only (no stock, no filters)
- Actual projects, actual clients
- Real light, real seasons
- Credit collaborators when shown (@practitioner, @host, @designer)
